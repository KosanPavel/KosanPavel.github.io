About Me

My introduction

Hi, I’m Pavel Košan.

I recently completed my Master’s degree in Software and Information Systems, focusing on Data Engineering and Big Data Analytics. I am now beginning my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering, with a focus on Data Science and Engineering in Biomedicine and Bioinformatics.

In my professional career, I work as a Data Analyst and BI Engineer, where I specialize in business intelligence, customer analytics (e.g., RFM and cohort analysis), and building and maintaining ETL/ELT data pipelines and complete analytics infrastructure. I also perform data mining on large-scale transactional datasets, and develop or orchestrate machine learning models using both custom code and cloud AI services such as Vertex AI Search for Retail.

As part of my academic focus, I’m deeply interested in advanced topics in machine and deep learning (e.g., graph neural networks), and in data science and engineering (e.g., similarity search and vector and graph databases). I’m also fascinated by the potential of AI agents and their applications in complex decision-making systems.

Outside of work and research, I enjoy diving into large fantasy and sci-fi novels — and I’m currently writing one of my own.

Bachelor Thesis

Deep Learning in Medical Applications

The aim of this work is to provide a basic description of deep learning and its possible use for the analysis of biomedical data. First, the fundamental theoretical concepts and algorithms on which contemporary deep learning is based are explained, then the important classes and architectures of deep neural networks are presented with the possibilities of their application in medicine. As part of the practical part, this work deals with three examples of deep learning applications for three different medical datasets. Special attention is paid to the last example which deals with segmentation of CT brain images.
